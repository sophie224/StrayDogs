Hi! I am Mura, a stray dog from Tbilisi. I am friendly to anyone who comes to pet me, but I dream to find my own home and lifelong friend one day. I spend my days with my friends, people feed us sometimes;

Mostly the owners of pizzeria give us some leftovers. But you can never know what will happen today. Join me today, a day that can decide my whole life.

Every day I wake up in the morning on the Freedom square, the first thing I think about is the foodstuff. I go down to the street where many shops and a huge restaurant is located. I adore the food this restaurant is serving

Every day I wake up in the morning in Rike park. The green atmosphere here is wonderful. My friends usually visit me in the morning and join me for breakfast. Sometimes it's easy to find a place for a meal, sometimes not

However there is always a chance to drive people’s hearts and make a hint with a facial expression that you need a care and a foodsponser. STAY IN THE STREET

Chapter Four

With all my heart I belong to the Freedom’s Square territory. Why? Because I love people and our society is mostly gathering in this place and bringing their warmth here

I would like to apply to those people who are working in the shelters: we dogs mostly want to have a place to live in winter, when it gets darker outside and people are afraid of us, however this doesn’t mean we want to be locked in one territory

Main dangers in the Street.

Main dangers for stray dogs are diseases. People refuse to vaccinate dogs, which leads to the spread of incredibly dangerous infections. These infections are not dangerous for humans.

I am talking about distemper, a porvil virus which is the leader of puppies and dogs. These are primary dangers. Other dangers include cars, accidents, starvation because there is not enough food available.

TO LIVE AT THE SHELTER

Chapter Five

When I am in the shelter my life completely changes. Day starts with morning cleaning, after this cleaning I feel very well. We eat our breakfast around 7-8 oclock and then our friend organizes morning exercises in the shelter. We go out for a minimum of 4-5 hours, but usually most of the day we spend in these groups in order not to be locked in the kennels.

Besides encouraging groups we are having meetings with volunteers. I adore taking morning exercises, because they give me positive energy and strengthen my body’s and face’s muscles for eating and hunting. And now it’s winter and at 5-6 o’clock we are back to the kennels, we receive tasty dinners and needed medications. At the end of the day we are having final cleanings and disinfections due to Covid.

SARA FROM DOG SHELTER

Chapter Eight

We work with many individuals and different organizations abroad. Those corporations help us send some of our dogs abroad once they don’t find their homes in Georgia. This is everywhere in the USA, in Germany, Poland and many countries.

Sarah's Tips for Adopting Dogs

People prefer adopting hybrid dogs, rather than adopting stray dogs. It is really difficult to explain that purebred dogs are genetically weaker than mixed stray dogs. Especially breeding in Georgia is very unregulated, very dangerous for dogs. I would like to encourage everyone to adopt mixed stray dogs instead of purebred onse

In Georgia the situation is different: very often people spend a lot of money to buy a dog that they are not able to handle, that’s why suddenly they end up in the street. This is why after 6 months of staying with dogs you become eligible to be adopted abroad as well.
